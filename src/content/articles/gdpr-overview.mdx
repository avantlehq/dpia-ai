# Understanding GDPR: A Complete Overview

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) represents one of the most significant changes to data protection law in decades. 

## What is GDPR?

GDPR is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy in the European Union and the European Economic Area. It also addresses the transfer of personal data outside the EU and EEA areas.

## Key Principles

1. **Lawfulness, fairness and transparency**
2. **Purpose limitation** 
3. **Data minimization**
4. **Accuracy**
5. **Storage limitation**
6. **Integrity and confidentiality**
7. **Accountability**

## When is a DPIA Required?

Under Article 35 of GDPR, a DPIA is required when processing is likely to result in high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, particularly in cases involving:

- Systematic and extensive evaluation of personal aspects
- Processing of special categories of data on a large scale
- Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas on a large scale
